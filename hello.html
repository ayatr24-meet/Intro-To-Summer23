Flamingo

How pretty they are!

Flamingos are distinctive wading birds with vibrant pink or reddish feathers, long legs, and curved bills, resulting from a diet rich in carotenoid pigments. Found in tropical and subtropical regions, they inhabit shallow lakes, lagoons, and tidal flats. Flamingos feed on algae, insects, and crustaceans, which they filter from the water. Highly social, they live in large colonies, perform synchronized displays for mating, and lay a single egg on mud mound nests. Conservation efforts focus on protecting their habitats from threats like pollution and habitat loss.
